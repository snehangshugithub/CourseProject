Chris Erickson studies various aspects of industrial relations in the local, national and world economies. His research focuses on comparative industrial relations systems, industrial relations and labor market transformation in different regions of the world, wage determination, collective bargaining, innovations in union organizing, skills development, outsourcing and remote work. Among his administrative roles, he has been senior associate vice provost and director of theUCLA International Institutefrom 2013-2021; faculty director of theUCLA International Education Officefrom 2016-17; faculty director oftheUCLA-NUS Executive MBAprogram from its inception in 2003 through 2012; Anderson School senior associate dean, global initiatives from 2007-2012 and founding director oftheCenter for Global Management;director of theUCLA Center for International Business Education and Researchfrom 2007-2012; associate director of theUCLA Institute for Research on Labor and Employment(previously the UCLA Institute of Industrial Relations) from 2001-2003, 2007-2008 and 2012-2013 and faculty director of theLabor and Workplace Studies Minorfrom 2004-2006 and 2012-2013; and Anderson School vice chair of the faculty from 1998-2002. He has taught at UCLA Anderson since 1991, and was previously a faculty member at the Cornell Industrial and Labor Relations School. He holds a Ph.D. in economics from MIT and a B.A. from Yale. He is also a graduate of John W. North, University Heights and Highland schools in Riverside, California.
