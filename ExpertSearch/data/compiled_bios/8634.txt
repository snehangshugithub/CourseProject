"Arts & Sciences Matters" podcast offers insights from our very own experts in the college, working on a range of social, cultural and scientific challenges. Kudos to Sandra Dwyer, winner of the 2019 American Philosophical Association (APA) Prize for Excellence in Philosophy Teaching. Many more awards, books, media appearances, grants and other accomplishments to check out. Previous Years20192018201720162019 December 2019 A look back at Arts & Sciences accomplishments and news highlights from 2019. Regents' Professor Jenny Yang named National Academy of Inventors Fellow. Must-read faculty books of 2019. November 2019 Arts & Sciences is helping to lead Georgia State's efforts to increase faculty diversity. Latest research discoveries. Georgia State's Nu Rho Psi chapter awarded Chapter of the Year by the National Honors Society in Neuroscience. October 2019 Strategic plan progress report published on new website. Arts & Sciences launches research podcast. The Institute for Women's, Gender & Sexuality Studies and the Department of Africana Studies commemorate 25 years. May 2019 Strategic plan implementation reaches one-year mark. GSUTV wins four Telly awards! Faculty and staff awards announced. Arts & Sciences faculty in the headlines. Nuclear physics group gets three major grants. March 2019 All about the College to Career initiative. Arts & Sciences announces recipients of the Deans Early Career Award. New books, grants and more. 2018 December 2018 Mission accomplished: Burning Bright campaign goals exceeded! The college announces new associate dean for research, innovation and graduate studies. New faculty publications to check out. Arts & Sciences faculty and students in the media. May 2018 Neuroscience Institute celebrates 10 years. Summer enrollment is back up! Kudos to faculty and staff awards recipients, new Distinguished University Professors, and recipients of several prestigious fellowships. Congrats to the Class of 2018! March 2018 Renewed focus on summer learning, kudos to graduate student Sushant Mahajan and others, international symposium on The Post-Obama Ethos: The Transnational U.S. in the Aftermath of Hope announced. February 2018 Implementation of the colleges new strategic plan begins, kudos to Todd Henry of Physics & Astronomy and others, 2018 Georgia Scientific Computing Symposium to be held on February 24. 2017 December 2017 The colleges new strategic plan, kudos to Ning Fang of Chemistry and others, Happy Holidays! November 2017 Come Back, Pounce Forward program announced, congratulations to Deans Early Career Award recipients, Nov 13-17 is International Education Week. October 2017 Preview of the Creative Media Industries Institute grand opening, 2017 Plummer Lecture, kudos to Pearl McHaney of English and others. August 2017 Beginning of year message for 2017-18, solar eclipse, kudos to Sharee Light of Psychology, Benoit Chassaing of Neuroscience, and others. May 2017 End of year message, a tribute to George Rainbolt of Philosophy. March 2017 A summary of the budget situation, staff awards announced. January 2017 Deans Office reorganization announced, kudos to Andrea Scarantino of Philosophy and Neuroscience. 2016 December 2016 Strategic planning message, kudos to Diane Belcher of Applied Linguistics, Samantha Terrie Parks of Biology, and others. November 2016 Dean Rosens first newsletter, new books from Brett Esaki of Religious Studies, Stephen Dobranski of English, and others.
