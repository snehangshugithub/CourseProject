Control Engineering, Chang'an University, 2003 Academic Positions Research Assistant Professor, Department of Mechanical Science and Engineering, University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign, Aug. 2015-date Postdoctoral Fellow, Department of Mechanical Science and Engineering, University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign, Jun. 2012-Aug. 2015 Visiting Scholar, Department of Mechanical Science and Engineering, University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign, Dec. 2009-Nov. 2011 Assistant Professor, Department of Computer and Information Engineering, HeNan University, July 2003-July 2005 Research Statement Professor Wang conducts research in heat transfer, fluid mechanics, and thermodynamics, with energy system applications, focused on single-phase and two-phase convection, phase change, heat transfer enhancement, systems, controls, diagnostics, and energy conversion efficiency. Her research has a scientific basis in the fundamentals of thermophysics and mathematics, with a deep appreciation of applications in energy generation and use. The overarching aim of her research program is to achieve a sustainable energy future. Research Interests Heat, mass, and momentum transport in multiphase flows for thermal desalination, scale crystallization and antifouling surfaces. Increasing energy efficiency (COP) of air-conditioning & refrigeration systems through improved component and system control, performance optimization, and new cycles. Fundamentals and application of fluid mechanics and thermal science in energy systems, including renewable energy, energy conversion, and power generation.
