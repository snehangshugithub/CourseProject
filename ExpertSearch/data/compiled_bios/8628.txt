It includes the theory of computing and the design of computer hardware and software as well as the application of computers to solving problems. Computer science is related to mathematics and engineering and draws many of its foundational principles from those fields. DID YOU KNOW? Since 2016, the Department of Computer Science has organized a hackathon known as HackGSU. There are five student organizations for computer science majors. Computer Science offers a dual-degree program that allows students to get both their bachelors and masters degrees in five years. Making a Difference Our department is nationally known for our research and the success of our graduates. We are widely recognized for our emphasis on interdisciplinary research, which runs the gamut from astroinformatics to bioinformatics to cybersecurity. Our student body is large and diverse and our graduates land jobs at top employers. Our program is one of the reasons top tech companies have launched major expansions in Atlanta. Microsoft has announced Atlanta will soon become one of its most important hubs. The company is building a campus in the citys Westside neighborhood and Google is moving into a new Midtown office tower. Explore Our Programs Undergraduate Programs We offer a bachelor of science degree in computer science, a bachelor of science degree in data science, certificates in cyber security and data science and a 5-year bachelors/masters program in computer science. MORE INFORMATION Graduate Programs We offer a master of science degree in computer science, a master of science degree in data science and analytics with a concentration in big data and machine learning and a Ph.D. in computer science. MORE INFORMATIONComputer Science by the Numbers $2 Million Average annual funding from research grants 36 Faculty Members 160+ Ph.D. graduates 2,400+ Undergraduate majors and 250+ graduate students 3,000+ Undergraduate alumni and 1,000+ graduate alumni Why Study Computer Science? Computer science is the gateway to the future. If you name any problem faced by humanity, it's likely computers will be the key to solving it. Computer scientists are trained to think logically and precisely and develop problem-solving skills, but creativity is a key element of computer science as well. Computer scientists increasingly work in interdisciplinary environments, so the field offers great opportunities for people with diverse interests and talents.
