Faculty Positions Policies Texas State College of Science and Engineering Ingram School of Engineering Contact Us Faculty Contacts Dr. Jitendra Tate Dr. Jitendra Tate Professor Ph.D., Mechanical Engineering, North Carolina A&T State University, 2004 M.S., Mechanical Engineering, University of Pune, 1996 B.S., Mechanical Engineering, University of Pune, 1990 Research Interests: Development, manufacturing; and characterization of high-performance thermoset and thermoplastics polymeric matrix composites for industrial, aerospace, sports, biomedical, and energy applications such as, High temperature applications e.g. rocket ablatives, Fire-retardant interior structures of mass transit and aircraft, Lighter and damage-tolerant wind turbine blades, Thermoplastics conductive composites for 3D printing, Bio-based composites using natural fibers (kenaf, ragweed, hemp, agricultural waste) and soy-based resins Characterization and progressive failure analysis of polymers, elastomers, and composites under mechanical loading (static, fatigue, and impact); thermal loading; FST (fire, smoke and toxicity); and ablation Manufacturing of thermoset and thermoplastics polymer matrix composites with special interest in low cost vacuum assisted resin transfer molding (VARTM) Fatigue of composites Life prediction modeling and damage mechanisms Structural health monitoring of composite structures Environmental, health and safety aspects of nanotechnology safety More Info:https://faculty.txstate.edu/profile/1922481 Top Ingram School of Engineering Ingram School of
