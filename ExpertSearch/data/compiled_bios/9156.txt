He holds a Ph.D. in Applied Science and Technology from the University of California, Berkeley (2015). He received the Dorothy M. and Earl S. Hoffman Scholarship from the American Vacuum Society in 2014, the Graduate Student Award from the Materials Research Society in 2015, the Chinese Government Award for Outstanding Self-Financed Students Abroad in 2015, the Beckman Postdoctoral Fellowship at UIUC during 2015-2018, the ACS PRF Doctoral New Investigator Award in 2019, and the Beckman Young Investigator Award from the Arnold & Mabel Beckman Foundation in 2021. Academic Positions Assistant Professor, Materials Science and Engineering, UIUC Faculty Affiliate, Materials Research Laboratory, UIUC Research Statement The Zhang group study the interfacial science of materials, with the goal of: 1) understanding the fundamental atomic scale processes at interfaces, such as charge storage, electrochemical transformation, and catalysis; 2) bringing solutions to the society, in areas of energy conversion and storage, information technology, and healthcare.Our research is highly interdisciplinary, spanning over areas of materials/physical chemistry, condensed matter physics, chemical engineering, and electrical engineering. We are always looking for motivated graduate students, postdocs, and undergrads to join us.
