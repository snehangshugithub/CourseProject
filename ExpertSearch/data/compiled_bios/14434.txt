Blatchley III, Ph.D., P.E., BCEE, F.ASCE Lee A. Rieth Professor in Environmental Engineering Purdue University Lyles School of Civil Engineering 550 Stadium Mall Drive West Lafayette, IN 47907-2051 Office: HAMP 2129 Phone: (765) 494-0316 Email: blatch@purdue.edu Web: personal web site CV: https://engineering.purdue.edu/CE/People/Faculty/Blatchley/Blatchley-CV-May-2021.pdf Specialty Area(s) Environmental Engineering Education Ph.D., University of California, Berkeley, 1988 M.S., University of California, Berkeley, 1983 B.S., Purdue University, 1981 Research Summary Physico/chemical processes of environmental engineering. Emphasis is on processes that relate to water quality, especially those that involve UV radiation, chlorine, or both. Current research foci include disinfection processes for water, UV-based advanced oxidation processes, and swimming pool chemistry Selected Research Projects 2012-present, US EPA, Hydrologists Helping Others (H2O), Dr. William M Scholl Foundation, Purdue University: "Water Supply in Developing Countries" (interdisciplinary service-learning program to provide community-scale water treatment systems to small communities in developing countries). 2012-present, Citizens Energy Group, WE&RF, Metropolitan Water Reclamation District of Greater Chicago, New York Department of Environmental Protection, NYSERDA, Suez Environnement: "Stochastic Methods for Design and Validation of UV Disinfection Systems." 2013-2014, Indiana Water Resources Research Center: "Attached-Growth Lagoons for Wastewater Nitrification in Small Communities." 2013-2015, NSF International: "Tetraselmis as a Challenge Organism for Validation of Ballast Water UV Systems." 2015-present, Trask Innovation Fund, Global Engineering Program Office, Borlaug Fellows Program: "Continuous-Flow, Community-Scale Solar UV Disinfection Systems for Water Treatment." 2011-2017, National Swimming Pool Foundation, China Scholarship Council: "Effects of Chlorine/UV Process on Air and Water Chemistry." 2015-present, China Scholarship Council, Purdue University: "Chlorine/UV Process for Degradation and Detoxification of Cyanotoxins." Honors & Awards EPA Training Fellowship, University of California, Berkeley, 1982 - 1983 Chi Epsilon, 1994 - present Harold Munson Outstanding Teacher Award, School of Civil Engineering, Purdue University, 1997 Roy E. and Myrna G. Wansik Research Leadership Award, School of Civil Engineering, Purdue University, 1998 William Edgar Award for Pioneering Research in Disinfection, Water Environment Federation, 2005 Diplomate Environmental Engineer (DEE) (aka, Board Certified Environmental Engineer, BCEE), American Academy of Environmental Engineers, by Eminence in the Specialty of Water Supply and Wastewater, 2006 - present Sigma Xi, 2007 - present Aquatics International 2008 "Power 25" - Annual list of the 25 most influential aquatics professionals (see http://www.aquaticsintl.com/2008/feb/0802_power.html) Fellow, American Society of Civil Engineers, 2014 - present Engagement and Service Award, Purdue University, College of Engineering, 2016. Awarded for leadership in development of interdisciplinary service-learning class entitled "Water Supply in Developing Countries." The class designs, builds, and implements holistic, community-scale water treatment systems for impoverished communities in developing countries.
