Audience Tools Search Menu Faculty > Yunhe Feng Yunhe Feng CV Postdoctoral Scholar yunhe@uw.edu Mary Gates Hall 310 Specializations Big Data Analytics, Mining, and Modeling Usable Privacy and Security for Mobile Devices Deep Learning for Social Good Research Areas Data Science Human-Computer Interaction Biography Yunhe Feng is a Postdoctoral Scholar with Professor Chirag Shah in the Information School at the University of Washington. Yunhe obtained the B.E. and M.E. degree from Beijing University of Technology, China, and the Ph.D. degree in Computer Science from the University of Tennessee, Knoxville, USA. His research interest lies in Data Mining and Knowledge Discovery, Mobile Usable Security and Privacy, Machine Learning, Human-Computer Interaction (HCI), Mobile Computing, and IoT. He has received several fellowships and awards, including the Min H. Kao Fellowship, UT EECS Department Excellence Fellowship, Kaggle's Open Data Research Grant Award, Google Cloud COVID-19 Research Credits Award, and a Best Paper Award by the IEEE BigDataSecurity 2018. Yunhe's research has been covered internationally by news outlets, including Financial Times, TNW, Business Insider, and Yahoo! Finance.
