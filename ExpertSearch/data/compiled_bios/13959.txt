Welty Professor of Mechanical Engineering Research Lab:Experimental Fluid Mechanics Research LabThermalFluid Sciences Education: PhD, Pennsylvania State University, 1976 MS, Stanford University, 1972 BS,Cornell University, 1971 Background: At OSU since 1998 Clemson University, 197897 Memphis University, 197678 United Technologies, 1972 Research Interests: Dr. Liburdy's research focuses on fundamental issues of turbulent flows, flow in porous media, enhancement of transport properties by complex flow structures, high speed droplet formation, thermal management, and low Reynolds number aerodynamics. Applications of this work include high heat flux micro-electronic cooling, high-temperature gas turbine blade cooling systems, micro-scale fluid flow systems for biological and chemical reactors and ink-jet printing technology, multi-phase ejector systems, and environmental control. Dr. Liburdy's research has received support from Hewlett-Packard, Xerox, Westinghouse, General Electric, AT&T, DuPont, Maxfli, and other industry sponsors as well as by DARPA, DoE, NSF, ONR, NASA, and AFOSR.
