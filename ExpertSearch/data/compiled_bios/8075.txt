These models are used to predict product quality, production rates and the effectiveness of operating and control strategies for industrial processes. Professor McAuley's models help companies to scale-up, monitor, optimize and control their production processes. One of the most difficult problems faced by modelers is determining appropriate values for kinetic, thermodynamic and mass-transfer parameters that appear in model equations. McAuley's research group has developed methods for estimating parameters in a variety of challenging situations including: models with a large number of parameters and insufficient data to reliably estimate all of them simplified models and models for use in on-line monitoring and control fundamental dynamic models for processes influenced bystochastic disturbances modelsof polymerization reactors thatdescribe detailed branching and molecular weight development Students are required for new research projects that focus on: selection of operating conditions for new experiments that will lead to improved parameter values and reliable model predictions combining imperfect fundamental models with data-driven disturbance models to obtain reliable predictions and process control Master'sand Ph.D. students who are interested in combining data with fundamental models of chemical processesare encouraged to apply.
